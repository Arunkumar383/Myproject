YANTRAS: SACRED GEOMETRY FOR ENLIGHTENMENT
Yantras

Yantras are intricate geometric designs used as powerful tools for meditation and spiritual awakening in Hinduism and Tantric traditions. These mystical diagrams are believed to embody cosmic forces and divine energies, serving as visual representations of the universe's structure. Each yantra typically consists of a complex arrangement of shapes, such as triangles, circles, and lotus petals, meticulously organized in symmetrical patterns.

The central point, or bindu, symbolizes the unity of all creation and the source of divine power. By focusing on a yantra during meditation, practitioners aim to harness its spiritual energy, quiet the mind, and achieve a deeper connection with the divine. Yantras are often inscribed on metals, paper, or sacred cloth and can be used in various rituals and ceremonies to attract positive influences, protect against negative energies, and enhance personal growth.
